## 1. Executive Summary

Between 2021 and 2025, the NFT market experienced a dramatic downturn. Dozens of NFT marketplaces and platforms shut down, each handling their sunset differently. This report analyzes **13 real-world cases** to extract best practices and anti-patterns for Lootex's own sunset announcement.

### Key Finding

The most well-received shutdowns share three traits:
1. **Transparency** — Clear, honest communication about what's happening and why
2. **Gratitude** — Genuine appreciation for the community
3. **Clarity on assets** — Explicit, upfront statement about what happens to user assets

**Lootex's critical advantage**: As a non-custodial marketplace, Lootex never held or custodied any user assets. This eliminates the most stressful concern users have during a platform sunset. The announcement should **lead with this fact**.

## 2. Research Methodology

We analyzed 13 NFT/Web3 project shutdowns across four categories:

| Category | Projects |

## 3. Case Studies

### 3.1 Formal Shutdown Announcements (Best Practices)

---

#### MakersPlace ⭐ **GOLD STANDARD**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Date** | December 20, 2024 (shutdown: January 28, 2025) |
| **Channels** | Email, blog post, Twitter/X, Discord |
| **Notice period** | ~5-6 weeks |
| **Asset model** | Hybrid custodial — users needed to withdraw NFTs |
| **Tone** | Grateful, empathetic, structured |

**Key announcement language:**
> *"After much deliberation, we have made the difficult decision to wind down MakersPlace operations."*
> *"It has been an incredible journey, and we want to thank our amazing community of artists and collectors for being part of this story."*
> *"We want to ensure a smooth transition for all of our community members."*

**What they did right:**
- Clear shutdown date announced upfront
- Step-by-step guides for NFT withdrawal
- Dedicated FAQ page
- Support staff remained available throughout wind-down
- Tools to export portfolio data
- Committed to maintaining IPFS pins post-shutdown

**Community reaction:** Sadness and nostalgia, but appreciation for the structured communication. Some frustration about the short timeline over the holidays.

---

#### Recur (Hello Kitty / Nickelodeon NFTs)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Date** | August 18, 2023 (platform deprecated November 16, 2023) |
| **Channels** | Twitter/X thread (@RecurForever) — primary channel; no dedicated blog post |
| **Notice period** | ~90 days, phased timeline |
| **Asset model** | Custodial — users needed to transfer to external wallets |
| **Tone** | Corporate, formal, euphemistic |
| **Funding raised** | $50M at $333M valuation |

**Key announcement language:**
> *"Today, with heavy hearts, we must share some difficult news."*
> *"After much contemplation and consideration, we've decided to deprecate the RECUR platform, effective November 16, 2023."*
> *"Unforeseen challenges and shifts in the business landscape have made it increasingly difficult for us to continue."*

**Phased timeline:**
- **Aug 18**: Primary/secondary sales disabled
- **Aug 31**: Deadline to withdraw USDC balances
- **Nov 9**: NFT deposits disabled
- **Nov 16**: Final deadline — all NFTs and USDC must be withdrawn
- **Nov 22**: NFT metadata migrated to IPFS/Filecoin

**What they did right:**
- 90-day phased wind-down with clear milestones
- Migrated 380,000+ NFT metadata to IPFS/Filecoin for permanent storage
- Kept platform operational during each phase

**What could have been better:**
- Language was overly corporate and euphemistic ("deprecate," "shifts in the business landscape")
- No alternative platform or migration partner offered

**Community reaction:** Shock and disillusionment. Recur Pass floor price collapsed from $88,888 high to ~$1.69. Media framed it as a cautionary tale — Gizmodo's headline: *"Godfather NFTs Weren't Enough to Prevent Recur From Sleeping With the Fishes."*

---

#### KnownOrigin (acquired by eBay)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Date** | February 2024 (acquired June 2022) |
| **Channels** | Email, Twitter/X, blog, eBay corporate comms |
| **Notice period** | ~30 days |
| **Asset model** | Non-custodial (Ethereum), but metadata hosted on servers |
| **Tone** | Corporate, impersonal |

**Key announcement language:**
> *"After careful consideration, we have made the difficult decision to wind down KnownOrigin."*

**What they did wrong:**
- Corporate, impersonal tone — stark contrast to the crypto-native community
- No migration to eBay's main platform
- Only ~18 months between acquisition and shutdown
- Artists felt abandoned: *"Our collector relationships, our sales history, our community — all gone"*

**Lesson for Lootex:** Even when the decision is difficult, **speak like a community member, not a corporation**. The KnownOrigin shutdown is a cautionary tale of tone-deaf communication.

### 3.2 Gradual Fade / No Formal Announcement (Anti-Patterns)

---

#### LooksRare

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Date** | Gradual decline 2023-2026 (still technically operational as of March 2026) |
| **Formal announcement** | **None** — no official shutdown post has ever been made |
| **Asset model** | Non-custodial |
| **Token** | $LOOKS at ~$0.0007 (market cap ~$691K); OKX delisted Nov 2025 |
| **Development** | ~1 developer, 7 weekly commits (DefiLlama, March 2025) |

**What went wrong:**
- No formal "we are shutting down" announcement was ever made
- The platform simply became less active — shifted from NFT trading rewards to gaming features (raffles, YOLO)
- Anonymous founders eroded trust
- Exchange delistings signal decline more loudly than any announcement
- Community felt abandoned with "rug pull" accusations

**Contrast with X2Y2:** LooksRare's "slow fade" model vs. X2Y2's "clean break" model. X2Y2's titled blog post *"The Sunset of X2Y2"* with a specific date was far better received.

**Lesson for Lootex:** A gradual fade is **always worse** than a clean, honest announcement. Silence breeds rumors and resentment. If you're shutting down, say it clearly.

---

#### Nifty Gateway (Gemini)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Date** | Gradual decline 2023-2025 |
| **Formal announcement** | No single shutdown announcement |
| **Asset model** | **Custodial** — users needed to withdraw |

**Critical issue:** Users with custodied NFTs face ongoing uncertainty. The gradual decline without clear deadlines left users confused about when/if to withdraw.

**Community reaction:**
> *"This is exactly why we need non-custodial solutions"*
> *"Not your keys, not your NFTs"*

**Lesson for Lootex:** Nifty Gateway's custodial model made their fade-out particularly damaging. Lootex's non-custodial model is a **massive advantage** — emphasize this prominently.

---

#### Async Art

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Date** | October 23, 2023 |
| **Channels** | Twitter/X thread by CEO Conlan Rios (@AsyncArt) |
| **Notice period** | ~2-3 months (through Jan 22, 2024) |
| **Asset model** | Non-custodial, BUT programmable features were platform-dependent |
| **Tone** | Personal, reflective, grateful — founder wrote as a community member |

**Key announcement language (CEO Conlan Rios):**
> *"Go where you're celebrated, not tolerated."*
> *"This has been a tough one, but the @AsyncArt team and I have made the hard decision to begin winding down operations."*
> *"Though this is the end of Async Art as a company, it's not the end of the dynamic and generative art movement that you've all helped us build."*

**What they did right:**
- All NFTs remain **pinned on IPFS** and on-chain
- Built a **"Classic Art Editor"** portal so users could continue interacting with programmable artworks
- Farewell message: *"Although we're saying goodbye, your art lives on as a testament to that."*
- Personal, community-native tone from the founder

**Community reaction:** Overwhelmingly **nostalgic and respectful** — described as "the end of an era." Artists posted heartfelt tributes. Relatively little anger because Rios communicated with genuine sincerity.

**Lesson for Lootex:** Even non-custodial platforms can have platform-dependent features. Clearly state what will and won't continue working after shutdown. Async Art's personal tone from the founder generated far better reception than Recur's corporate language.

---

#### Quixotic (Optimism L2)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Date** | Rebranded to Stratos (Aug 2022), then Stratos also died (2023) |
| **Formal announcement** | Rebrand announced; subsequent death was silent |

**Lesson for Lootex:** Don't rebrand as a last-ditch effort. If you're shutting down, be direct.

### 3.3 Pivots Framed as Evolution

---

#### X2Y2 ($5.6B lifetime volume)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Date** | March 31, 2025 (shutdown: April 30, 2025) |
| **Channels** | Blog on **Mirror.xyz** + **Paragraph**, amplified via Twitter/X (@the_x2y2), founder TP's personal account |
| **Notice period** | 30 days |
| **Tone** | Reflective, forward-looking — "clean break + pivot" |
| **Blog title** | *"The Sunset of X2Y2"* |

**Key language (founder TP):**
> *"After 3 incredible years and $5.6B in trading volume, X2Y2 will be sunsetting as an NFT marketplace on April 30, 2025."*
> *"Marketplaces live or die by network effects. We fought tooth and nail to be #1, but after three years, it's clear it's time to move on."*
> *"The NFT chapter taught us a lot — most of all, that lasting value beats chasing trends. That lesson's why we're drawing a line here, not a pause or a maybe, but a full stop on X2Y2 as we knew it."*

**Asset handling:**
- Non-custodial — NFTs safe in user wallets
- Smart contracts left live: *"The smart contracts will keep running, so you can still interact with them as needed — no rush to pull anything."*
- Security experts recommended users **revoke contract approvals** to avoid post-shutdown phishing

**Community reaction:** $X2Y2 token dropped 10-13% on announcement (already down 99.6% from ATH of ~$4.17 to ~$0.0014). Shutdown seen as inevitable. Some skepticism about AI pivot as "narrative hop."

**Lesson for Lootex:** X2Y2's "clean break" with a titled blog post, specific volume stats, and honest reflection was received far better than a gradual fade. The "full stop, not a pause or a maybe" framing was clear and respected.

**Sources:** [Mirror.xyz](https://mirror.xyz/x2y2.eth/85DG2KcKmJd1AiyXU7BU2yATys1hgxWWXju_pmESwYI) | [CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2025/03/31/nft-marketplace-x2y2-to-shut-down-after-trading-volumes-collapsed) | [The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/348866/nft-marketplace-x2y2-shut-down-crypto-ai-pivot)

#### Mintable

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Date** | September 2023 onward |
| **Framing** | "Transitioning to zkSync" |
| **Critical issue** | Gasless/lazy-minted NFTs that were never purchased **effectively disappeared** |

**Key language:**
> *"We're evolving Mintable to bring a better experience."*

**What went wrong:**
- The "upgrade" framing was misleading — it was essentially a restart
- No migration of existing NFTs to the new platform
- Users with gasless mints lost their creations

**Lesson for Lootex:** Be honest. If the marketplace is ending, say "ending," not "evolving."

---

#### Gem.xyz → OpenSea Pro ⭐ **BEST TRANSITION**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Date** | Acquired April 2022, merged September 2023 |
| **Channels** | OpenSea blog, Twitter, email, in-app notifications |
| **Notice period** | ~5-6 months |
| **Migration** | Seamless — user settings and features migrated to OpenSea Pro |

**Key language:**
> *"We're taking everything you love about Gem and bringing it to OpenSea Pro."*
> *"OpenSea Pro is the evolution of Gem — built for pro traders."*

**Why it worked:** Gem was an aggregator (no asset custody), the team continued, features were preserved, and users got a working successor product. The transition was framed as an **upgrade**, and it genuinely was one.

**Lesson for Lootex:** If there's a natural successor platform or partner, recommend it. Even without a formal migration, suggesting alternatives shows you care.

---

#### Foundation (still operating)

Foundation never shut down but pivoted repeatedly (invite-only → open → Worlds). Each change was framed as "evolution." This avoided negative headlines but caused identity confusion.

**Lesson:** The "continuous evolution" approach only works if you actually continue operating.

### 3.4 Abrupt / Dramatic Shutdowns

---

#### hic et nunc (Tezos)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Date** | November 11, 2021 |
| **Announcement** | Twitter bio changed to **"discontinued"** + one tweet with the smart contract address |
| **Notice period** | **Zero** — immediate, no warning |
| **Asset model** | Non-custodial (Tezos blockchain + IPFS) |
| **Scale at shutdown** | 500,000+ NFTs, ~30,000 active wallets, $50.37M total sales |

**What happened:** Founder Rafael Lima unilaterally took down hicetnunc.xyz with no warning. An artist reported he "got overwhelmed and decided, overnight, to close the site." No explanation, no farewell message, no instructions for users.

**No assets lost:** All NFTs remained on the Tezos blockchain, all media files on IPFS. The shutdown was purely a frontend removal — smart contracts continued functioning.

**Remarkable community response:** Within days, multiple forks emerged:
- **objkt.com** — Immediately absorbed HEN traffic, became the largest Tezos NFT marketplace
- **Teia (teia.art)** — Community-built fork governed as a DAO
- **hicetnunc.art** — Mirror site later handed to the community
- Lima reduced marketplace contract fee from 2.5% to 1% and ceded control to the community

**Lesson for Lootex:** While HEN's abruptness is an anti-pattern, the community's response shows the power of non-custodial, on-chain assets. Open-source code + on-chain contracts = community can resurrect even after founder departure.

---

#### Cent (~150,000 users, home of Jack Dorsey's $2.9M tweet NFT)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Date** | February 6, 2022 (not 2023 as sometimes misreported) |
| **Announcement** | **Reuters** exclusive interview with CEO Cameron Hejazi (not public announcement first) |
| **Action** | Halted most NFT trading; "Valuables" (tweet NFTs) continued |
| **Advance notice** | Zero — users discovered trading was disabled before any explanation |

**Remarkably candid key quotes (Reuters interview):**
> *"There's a spectrum of activity that is happening that basically shouldn't be happening — like, legally."*
> *"We would ban offending accounts but it was like we're playing a game of whack-a-mole... Every time we would ban one, another one would come up, or three more would come up."*
> *"I think this is a pretty fundamental problem with Web3."*

**Three problems cited:** (1) selling unauthorized copies of other NFTs, (2) creating NFTs of content that doesn't belong to the creator, (3) selling NFT sets that resemble securities.

**Community reaction:** Became a landmark moment in the NFT plagiarism/fraud discourse. Cent's honesty was **both respected and weaponized** — NFT skeptics cited Hejazi's quotes as industry-wide indictment; the crypto community appreciated the candor. The marketplace appears to have never fully reopened.

**Lesson for Lootex:** Honesty about industry challenges is respected when done right, but be careful about statements that could be used to undermine the broader ecosystem. Also: announce to your own community first, not through media interviews.

## 5. Key Patterns & Lessons Learned

### DO ✅

1. **Lead with asset safety** — The #1 user concern is "what happens to my NFTs/money?" Answer this immediately. Lootex's non-custodial model means the answer is simple and reassuring.

2. **Be honest and direct** — Say "we are shutting down" or "sunsetting." Don't use euphemisms like "evolving" or "pivoting" if there's no genuine continuation.

3. **Express genuine gratitude** — Every well-received announcement included heartfelt thanks. MakersPlace and Recur excelled here.

4. **Provide a clear timeline** — State exactly when the platform will stop functioning. Ambiguity causes anxiety.

5. **Multi-channel announcement** — Use ALL channels simultaneously: blog post, Twitter/X, email, Discord. Don't let users hear about it secondhand.

6. **Provide FAQ** — MakersPlace's dedicated FAQ page was praised. Anticipate questions and answer them proactively.

7. **Suggest alternatives** — Even without a formal migration, recommending other marketplaces shows goodwill.

8. **Keep smart contracts accessible** — Remind users that on-chain contracts continue to function independently of the platform.

### DON'T ❌

1. **Don't go silent** — LooksRare and Nifty Gateway's gradual fades generated the worst community reactions. Silence breeds rumors.

2. **Don't use corporate speak** — KnownOrigin's eBay-style announcement felt cold to a crypto-native community.

3. **Don't pretend it's a pivot** — X2Y2's "AI pivot" and Mintable's "zkSync upgrade" were seen as dishonest.

4. **Don't leave custodied assets in limbo** — Not applicable to Lootex, but a critical lesson from Nifty Gateway.

5. **Don't skip the human element** — The best announcements included personal reflections from founders, not just corporate statements.

6. **Don't announce only through media** — Cent's announcement via The Verge interview before telling their own community first was criticized.

7. **Don't shut down abruptly** — hic et nunc's zero-notice shutdown caused initial shock and anger.

## 6. Lootex-Specific Advisory

### Lootex's Unique Position

Lootex has a **significant communication advantage** over most projects in this report:

| Factor | Lootex | Most Other Projects |
|--------|--------|---------------------|
| Asset custody | **Non-custodial** | Custodial or hybrid |
| User action required | **None** | Withdraw, transfer, backup |
| Asset risk | **Zero** | Low to high |
| Complexity of message | **Simple** | Complex |

This means Lootex's announcement can be **shorter, simpler, and less stressful** than most competitors' shutdowns.

### Recommended Announcement Strategy

#### Channel Prioritization

| Priority | Channel | Purpose |
|----------|---------|---------|
| 1 | **Blog/website** | Full announcement with FAQ |
| 2 | **Twitter/X** | Concise announcement + link to blog |
| 3 | **Email** | Direct notification to registered users |
| 4 | **Discord** | Community Q&A, personal touch |
| 5 | **Telegram** (if applicable) | Additional reach |

#### Timing

- **Announce on a weekday** (Tuesday-Thursday) — weekends get less attention
- **Publish all channels simultaneously** — don't let users discover it secondhand
- **Provide 2-4 weeks notice** before platform goes offline (longer is better if possible)

#### Tone Guide

Based on the best-performing announcements (MakersPlace, Recur):

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|--------|----------|
| Warm, genuine, personal | Corporate, formal, distant |
| "We're grateful" | "After careful consideration" |
| Acknowledge the journey | Focus only on the ending |
| "Your NFTs are safe" | Avoid mentioning assets |
| Name specific milestones | Keep it generic |
| Sign from the team/founder | Anonymous corporate post |

## 7. Recommended Announcement Template

Below is a recommended structure based on best practices from 13 case studies. Adapt language and details as needed.

---

### Blog Post / Full Announcement

```
Title: A Message to the Lootex Community

[Opening — Lead with gratitude]
To our Lootex community,

After [X years] of building Lootex, we are writing to share that we will be
sunsetting the Lootex NFT marketplace on [DATE].

This was not an easy decision. [Brief, honest reason — e.g., "The NFT marketplace
landscape has changed significantly, and after careful consideration, we've decided
to wind down operations."]

[Asset safety — THE most important paragraph]
Important: Your NFTs are safe. Lootex has never held or custodied any user assets.
All NFTs remain in your own wallets, fully under your control. You do not need to
take any action. Your assets are on-chain and are not affected by this change.

[Timeline]
Here's what to expect:
- [DATE]: This announcement
- [DATE]: Marketplace listing/trading features will be disabled
- [DATE]: The Lootex website will go offline
- Smart contracts on-chain will continue to function independently

[Gratitude — Be specific]
We want to thank every artist, collector, and community member who was part of
the Lootex journey. [Include 1-2 specific milestones or memories that are
meaningful.]

[Alternatives — Show goodwill]
Your NFTs can continue to be viewed and traded on other marketplaces such as
[list 2-3 alternatives relevant to the chains Lootex supported].

[FAQ section]
Frequently Asked Questions:

Q: Do I need to withdraw my NFTs?
A: No. Lootex never held your NFTs. They are in your wallet and fully under
   your control.

Q: Will my NFTs still exist?
A: Yes. Your NFTs are on the blockchain and exist independently of Lootex.
   They are not affected by this change.

Q: Can I still trade my NFTs?
A: Yes. You can trade your NFTs on any compatible marketplace.

Q: What happens to listed items on Lootex?
A: All active listings will be cancelled when marketplace features are disabled
   on [DATE]. No pending transactions will be affected.

Q: When will the website go offline?
A: The Lootex website will remain accessible until [DATE].

[Closing — Personal, from the team]
Thank you for being part of Lootex. Building this platform and serving this
community has been [personal reflection].

With gratitude,
The Lootex Team
```

## 8. Communication Checklist

### Pre-Announcement

- [ ] Draft blog post, tweet, email, and Discord message
- [ ] Internal team alignment — everyone knows before it goes public
- [ ] Prepare FAQ page
- [ ] Identify 2-3 alternative marketplaces to recommend
- [ ] Brief any partners/collaborators before public announcement
- [ ] Set exact dates for each milestone (listing disable, site offline)

### Announcement Day

- [ ] Publish blog post first
- [ ] Post Twitter/X thread immediately after
- [ ] Send email blast simultaneously
- [ ] Post in Discord with @everyone tag
- [ ] Pin announcement in all channels
- [ ] Founder/team available for Discord Q&A (first 24-48 hours)

### Post-Announcement

- [ ] Monitor social media for questions and respond promptly
- [ ] Update website banner/homepage with sunset notice
- [ ] Keep FAQ updated with new questions that arise
- [ ] Send reminder email 1 week before site goes offline
- [ ] Disable marketplace features on stated date
- [ ] Take site offline on stated date (or redirect to a static goodbye page)
- [ ] Consider leaving a static page with FAQ accessible for 3-6 months

### What to Keep Accessible After Shutdown

- [ ] Static FAQ page (hosted cheaply, e.g., GitHub Pages)
- [ ] Smart contract documentation (so users know which contracts are theirs)
- [ ] IPFS metadata (if Lootex pinned any — ensure pins are maintained or transferred)
