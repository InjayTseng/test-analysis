# Part II: The Building - Taipei 101

## 2.1 Physical Specifications

| Specification | Value |
|--------------|-------|
| **Total Height** | 508m (1,667 ft) to spire tip |
| **Roof Height** | 448m (1,470 ft) |
| **Floors** | 101 above ground |
| **Structure** | 8 stacked "bamboo" segments of 8 floors each |
| **Exterior** | Double-paned glass curtain wall, aluminum frames |
| **Design Wind Load** | 216 km/h (134 mph) |
| **Tuned Mass Damper** | 730 tons, floors 87-92 |

## 2.2 Climbing-Relevant Architecture

**The Bamboo Segments (Floors 27-90):**
- Eight 8-floor modules with **outward-flaring angles** (7-degree tilt)
- Creates ~64 floors of overhanging climbing
- Segment transitions provide **rest ledges** every 35 meters
- "Ruyi" decorative ornaments (8m+ tall) at segment junctions

**Surface Analysis:**
- Primary grip points: Aluminum mullion frames (50-75mm width)
- Glass panels: Molecularly smooth, near-zero friction
- Estimated grip availability: **10-15x worse** than natural granite

**The Spire (448m-508m):**
- 60-meter steel lattice structure
- More grip points than glass facade
- Extreme wind exposure
- Contains two 6-ton tip dampers

# Part V: Critical Risk Factors

## 5.1 Weather Analysis

**Taipei January Climate:**
- Average temperature: 13-19°C (optimal for climbing)
- Humidity: 77-78% (high; affects grip)
- Precipitation: 100mm average (12-14 rain days)
- Wind: Northeast monsoon season; variable

**Weather Impact on Time:**
- Rain: +100% time increase (Robert's 2004 experience)
- High wind (>30 km/h): +25-50% time increase
- Humidity >80%: +10-20% time increase

## 5.2 Physiological Limits

**Grip Strength Degradation:**
- After 24 hours of climbing: 14.7-15.1% grip strength loss
- Static hang time loss: 71.2% after exhaustive climbing
- Recovery requires 20+ minutes; incomplete during continuous climb

**Fatigue Timeline:**
| Duration | Grip Capacity | Performance Impact |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| 0-1 hour | 95-100% | Minimal |
| 1-2 hours | 85-95% | Noticeable |
| 2-3 hours | 75-85% | Significant |
| 3-4 hours | 65-75% | Substantial |
| 4+ hours | <65% | Critical |

## 5.3 Surface Reliability Concerns

- Glass panels: Near-zero friction; engineered for smoothness
- Aluminum frames: 50-75mm width; primary grip points
- Thermal expansion: Metal frames change dimension with temperature
- Condensation: Glass surfaces slick in high humidity
